Mood disorders

Mood disorders are diagnoses that have been classified through a lot of research and thought, but keep in mind that a diagnosis may or may not be the most accurate description of your current state, or remain a permanent diagnosis. Moods can be transient, and therefore diagnoses can shift over time.

The information for the content of this project are derived from the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders, 5th Edition, Text Revision, also known as DSM-V-TR, a manual published by the American Psychiatric Association (APA) that includes all currently recognized mental health disorders, UpToDate, and other reputable clinical sources. DSM-V codes are used by healthcare professionals to describe and identify the features of a given mental disorder, and to indicate how the disorder can be distinguished from other, similar problems. From this information, a psychologist or psychiatrist can diagnose a patient, and a licensed doctor can prescribe the necessary medications.
