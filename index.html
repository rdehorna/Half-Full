Being diagnosed with a mood disorder such as major depression or bipolar disorder can seem life­ altering. However, what does this diagnosis actually mean and how does it impact your life?

Our initial pilot application targets pre­-professional and professional populations where it is necessary to be a high­ functioning person in day­-to-­day life to perform your job. For some, depression and certain mood disorders are more easily treatable with options such as counseling, cognitive behavioral therapy, or medications that are only needed short­term. For others, depression can be a life-long struggle that never seems to fully go away, and for which there seems to be no definitive treatments (or long­-lasting solutions).

However, one step towards understanding and management of a mood disorder can be as simple as mood tracking​. As we are all unique people, a diagnosis often only begins to identify the tip of the iceberg and describe the possible reasons why you may be feeling the way you are. The reasons for a depressed mood could be changes in brain neurotransmitters, neuroanatomy, important life events, genetics, or other external/internal factors that result in short­term to long­term conditions. In reality, a person’s mood is complicated and consists of a mix of emotions, ideas, and thoughts. There is no black and white when it comes to how you are feeling, as there can be many shades of color in between. The point is: mood is ever­-changing and transient.

More importantly, understanding your own mood and seeing the trends over time can offer you more options to take control of your own mood, and to help you identify what you need in order to feel better. This analysis can even act as a contingency plan to alert you, your loved ones, or your registered physician (if you should choose) that you may need some help. This help may be changes in medications or in lifestyle. Either way, you will have another option to better take control of your mood.

Have you ever downloaded one of those depression apps that asks you to rate your mood or feelings on a scale of 1­ to­ 10, or to use a slider bar between opposite words such as “Depressed” and “Happy” or “Angry” and “Jovial”? Are you tired of this? Take control of your life today by receiving texts/notifications every 2­ to 3 days, and having your friends, family, or a kindred pre­-screened soul in the health professions help you keep track of how you are doing.
